## What is it?

"Revenge porn" is when someone shares — or threatens to share — intimate photos or videos of you without your permission. The term "revenge porn" is misleading because the images don't have to be sexual (just intimate), you don't have to have shared them originally for it to be a crime, and the motivation doesn't have to be "revenge" — it's always illegal without consent. The Online Safety Act 2023 significantly strengthened the law. Even **threatening to share** images is now a criminal offence.

## Is this a crime?

**Yes.** Sharing or threatening to share intimate images without consent is a criminal offence under the Online Safety Act 2023.

## The law says...

Online Safety Act 2023 — it is an offence to share, or threaten to share, intimate photographs or films of a person without their consent, where this is done to cause distress or the person is reckless as to whether distress is caused.

## To prove this, police need to show:

1. A photograph or film was shared (or threatened to be shared)
2. It showed private sexual activity or genitalia
3. The person in the image did not consent to the sharing
4. The suspect intended to cause distress (or was reckless about causing it)

## Signs this may be happening to you

- An ex-partner or someone else has shared or is threatening to share intimate images of you
- Intimate images of you have appeared online without your permission
- Someone is using intimate images to control, blackmail, or harass you
- You are receiving messages from strangers who have seen intimate images of you

## What to do right now

1. **Do not delete evidence** — screenshot the post, the URL, the account that shared it
2. **Report to police** — call 101 or visit your local station with your evidence
3. **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline** (0345 6000 459) — they help remove images from platforms
4. **Go to StopNCII.org** to create a digital fingerprint to prevent future uploads
5. **Report to the platform directly** — most have specific reporting for intimate images
6. **Consider contacting a solicitor** for an emergency injunction if images are spreading

## Maximum sentence

- **Sharing intimate images:** up to 2 years
- **Sharing with intent to cause distress:** up to 2 years
- **Threatening to share:** up to 2 years

## Common myths — the truth

| Myth | Truth |
|------|-------|
| "I sent the photos, so it's my fault" | Sharing an image with one person does not give them permission to share it with anyone else |
| "It's only a crime if they do it for revenge" | The motivation doesn't matter — sharing without consent is always illegal |
| "Nothing can be done once images are online" | The Revenge Porn Helpline has a high success rate in getting images removed |
