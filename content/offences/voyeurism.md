## What is it?

Voyeurism is when someone secretly watches you doing something private for their own sexual gratification. Upskirting — taking a photo or video up someone's skirt or kilt without permission — became a specific criminal offence in April 2019 following campaigning by Gina Martin. Hidden cameras in bathrooms, changing rooms, or bedrooms are also covered.

## Is this a crime?

**Yes.** Both voyeurism and upskirting are criminal offences.

## The law says...

Sexual Offences Act 2003, Section 67 covers voyeurism. The Voyeurism (Offences) Act 2019 specifically criminalises upskirting — operating equipment beneath a person's clothing without consent.

## To prove upskirting, police need to show:

1. The suspect operated equipment beneath a person's clothing
2. Without that person's consent
3. To observe genitalia or buttocks, OR to obtain sexual gratification, OR to humiliate/distress the victim

## To prove voyeurism (s.67), police need to show:

1. The suspect observed another person for the purpose of sexual gratification
2. The other person was doing a private act
3. The suspect knew the other person did not consent to being observed

## Signs this may be happening to you

- You discover a hidden camera in your bathroom, bedroom, or changing area
- Someone has taken photos or video up your skirt without permission
- You notice someone using their phone suspiciously near you on public transport or in crowds
- Someone has secretly filmed you during intimate moments

## What to do right now

1. **If you discover a hidden camera — do NOT touch or move it** — photograph it in place
2. **Call police immediately** (999 if you feel in danger, 101 otherwise)
3. **Note the exact location and time**
4. **If someone upskirted you, try to note their appearance** and report to police
5. **Contact Victim Support** (0808 168 9111) for confidential help

## Maximum sentence

- **Upskirting:** up to 2 years (up to 5 if placed on Sex Offenders Register)
- **Voyeurism:** up to 2 years

## Common myths — the truth

| Myth | Truth |
|------|-------|
| "It's not a big deal — they only looked" | Voyeurism is a criminal offence that violates your privacy and dignity |
| "Upskirting is just a prank" | It is a specific criminal offence carrying up to 2 years in prison |
