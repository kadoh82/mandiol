## What is it?

Sexual harassment is unwanted behaviour of a sexual nature. It can happen in the street, in a bar, online, or at work. Since the Criminal Justice Act 2023, street harassment is a specific criminal offence in England & Wales. You don't have to have said "stop" for something to be harassment — if it was unwanted and sexual, it qualifies.

## Is this a crime?

**Yes.** Street sexual harassment is a criminal offence under the Criminal Justice Act 2023. Workplace sexual harassment is unlawful under the Equality Act 2010, and your employer has a legal duty to protect you.

## The law says...

Criminal Justice Act 2023 — street sexual harassment is a specific criminal offence. The Equality Act 2010 and Worker Protection Act 2023 require employers to take reasonable steps to prevent workplace sexual harassment.

## To prove street sexual harassment, police need to show:

1. The suspect engaged in behaviour that was unwanted
2. The behaviour was of a sexual nature
3. It occurred in a public space
4. The victim was caused alarm, distress or humiliation (or this was intended)

## Workplace harassment

Your employer has a legal duty to protect you from sexual harassment at work. The Worker Protection Act 2023 strengthened this duty — employers must now take "reasonable steps" to prevent harassment. You can bring a claim at an Employment Tribunal.

## Signs this may be happening to you

- Catcalling, wolf-whistling, or sexual comments directed at you in the street
- Someone following you while making sexual comments
- Unwanted sexual comments, jokes, or touching at work
- Receiving unsolicited sexual images
- Sexual comments or messages on social media
- Someone making you feel unsafe through sexual behaviour

## What to do right now

1. **If you feel unsafe, move to a place with other people** or call 999
2. **Note what happened** — date, time, location, description of the person, any witnesses
3. **Report street harassment to police** — call 101 (non-emergency) or report online
4. **For workplace harassment, keep a written record** and contact ACAS (0300 123 1100)
5. **Contact Victim Support** (0808 168 9111) for confidential help

## Maximum sentence

Varies depending on the specific offence and context.

## Common myths — the truth

| Myth | Truth |
|------|-------|
| "It's just a compliment" | If it's unwanted and sexual in nature, it's harassment — not flattery |
| "You have to say stop first" | You do not need to tell someone to stop for their behaviour to be harassment |
| "It only counts if it's physical" | Verbal and online sexual harassment are also offences |
