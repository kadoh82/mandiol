## What is it?

Sexual assault is any intentional sexual touching of your body without your consent. This includes grabbing or groping any part of your body, rubbing against you sexually in a crowd, kissing you without consent, forcing you to touch someone sexually, or touching over clothing. It does NOT require penetration. It does NOT require violence. Any unwanted sexual touch counts.

## Is this a crime?

**Yes.** Sexual assault is a criminal offence regardless of whether it involved violence or penetration.

## The law says...

Sexual Offences Act 2003, Section 3 — it is an offence for a person intentionally to touch another person, where the touching is sexual, the complainant does not consent, and the defendant does not reasonably believe they consent.

## To prove this, police need to show:

1. The suspect intentionally touched another person
2. The touching was sexual
3. The complainant did not consent to the touching
4. The suspect did not reasonably believe the complainant consented

## Signs this may be happening to you

- Someone has touched you sexually without your permission
- You have been groped, grabbed, or kissed without consenting
- Someone rubbed against you sexually in a public place
- You were pressured or forced to touch someone else sexually
- Someone touched you over or under your clothing without agreement

## What to do right now

1. **Remove yourself from the situation if you can** — your safety comes first
2. **Tell someone you trust** — a friend, family member, or support worker
3. **Write down what happened** — include date, time, location, and any witnesses
4. **Contact Rape Crisis** (0808 802 9999) or **Victim Support** (0808 168 9111)
5. **You can report to police when you feel ready** — there is no time limit

## Maximum sentence

**10 years imprisonment**

## Common myths — the truth

| Myth | Truth |
|------|-------|
| "It was just a grope — it's not that serious" | Any unwanted sexual touching is a criminal offence carrying up to 10 years in prison |
| "I didn't say no, so it's not assault" | Consent must be actively given — silence or not resisting does not mean consent |
| "It happened in a club, everyone does it" | Crowded places do not make sexual touching legal |
