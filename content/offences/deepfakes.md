## What is it?

A deepfake is a realistic fake image or video created using AI that puts your face onto another body — often in sexual situations. This can be done using only ordinary photos taken from your social media, without you ever posing for any sexual image. Sharing intimate deepfakes without consent is a criminal offence under the Online Safety Act 2023. The Criminal Justice Bill 2024 goes further: it will criminalise the **creation** of intimate deepfakes, even if they are never shared.

## Is this a crime?

**Yes.** Sharing intimate deepfakes without consent is already a criminal offence. Creating them is being criminalised under new legislation.

## The law says...

Online Safety Act 2023 — sharing intimate deepfake images without consent is a criminal offence. The Criminal Justice Bill 2024 extends this to criminalise the creation of intimate deepfakes.

## To prove sharing (Online Safety Act 2023), police need to show:

1. A deepfake intimate image was shared
2. The person depicted did not consent
3. The suspect had no reasonable belief the person consented

## Signs this may be happening to you

- You discover realistic sexual images of yourself that you never posed for
- Someone is threatening to create or share AI-generated intimate images of you
- Friends or strangers contact you about intimate images you don't recognise
- Someone is using deepfake images to harass, blackmail, or humiliate you

## What to do right now

1. **Screenshot the image, the URL, and the account** — preserve evidence first
2. **Report to police** — call 101 or visit your local station
3. **Report to the platform** — request emergency removal
4. **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline** (0345 6000 459) — they assist with deepfakes
5. **Contact a solicitor** for an emergency injunction if the image is spreading

## Maximum sentence

Currently up to **2 years** for sharing intimate deepfakes. The law in this area is developing rapidly.

## Common myths — the truth

| Myth | Truth |

| "It's not real, so it's not a crime" | Sharing intimate deepfakes without consent is a criminal offence regardless |

## Note on evolving law

This area of law is developing rapidly. The government has committed to criminalising the creation of intimate deepfakes. Always check current guidance at legislation.gov.uk or with a specialist solicitor.
